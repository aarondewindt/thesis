The technological advancements and the steadily increasing demand for air and space travel of the past decades have lead to the development of new, more complex aerospace systems, resulting in new challenges in the field of aerospace control. At the same time, advancements in automated control have been seen in many domains, ranging from industrial manufacturing and household appliances to the rapid widespread adoption of UAVs. However, most of these systems are only able to complete their tasks within the narrow, predictable conditions they where designed for. Autonomous control systems need to be able to adapt to unexpected situations and environments. This has motivated researchers to search for new innovative methods that can be used to solve these new problems.

One of the leading causes of fatal air traffic accidents is loss of control. If fully autonomous UAV's are to be used in the future, these need to be at least as safe as a human piloted aircraft. However since it is impossible to take every known and unknown failure case into account when designing flight control systems, it is necessary to create fault tolerant control systems capable of identifying and adapting to changing conditions.

Furthermore, thanks to the miniaturization of technologies, Micro Air Vehicles are becoming smaller, more diverse and are seeing a rise is use. However, these types of vehicles come with their own new problems. In order to design a reliable, well performing control system, an accurate dynamics model is needed. This is not always possible for MAVs because of the complex nature of MAV flight dynamics, inconsistent manufacturing and parameter adaptation during time. Because of their smaller size, MAVs are more susceptible to unforseen disturbances and have sensor and actuator limitations due to their weight and size requirements.

Adaptive control methods allow autonomous systems to reconfigure them self if necessary. This opens the possibility to create fault tolerant control systems and control systems capable of determining the governing dynamics and track changes that may occur over time due to degradation or varying flight conditions. These systems can also detect and compensate for the limited on-board resources and any inconsistencies that may have occurred during manufacturing.

Reinforcement learning is a framework of intelligent, self-learning methods that can be applied to a wide range of autonomous operations and applications. These methods have been used in robotics for decades and a lot of research has been taking place at the same time in a wide range of topics, from resource management in computer clusters to optimizing chemical reactions. However, most of these use cases involve taking high level decisions such as trajectories, schedules, etc, and not low level actuator control.
