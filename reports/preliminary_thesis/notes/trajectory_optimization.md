Trajectory optimization (TO) is a set of mathematical techniques used to find the ideal or desired behavior of a dynamical system.

Class of method used to solve optimal control problem.


Optimal control is concerned the best choice of controls to achieve some desired goal.
Closed-loop
 - u = u(x)
 - global method and solution
 - More difficult to compute.
 - Only on lower dimensional problems
Open-loop
 - u = u(t)
 - Local method and solutions 
 - Easier
 - Can be used on higher dimensional problems.

 Transcription
  Transform the trajecotry optimization problem into a contraint parameter optimization problem. -> Non-Linear problem (NLP)

  Difference between TO and NLP
   - In TO, decision variables are vector functions
     In NLP decision variables are real numbers
   - TO is infinitely dimensional, NLP is finite dimensional
   - TO uses differential equations. NLP algebraic equations.
   - The bulk of the problem with TO is transcription. Transforming a TO into NLP.
