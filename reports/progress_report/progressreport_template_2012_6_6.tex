	\begin{appendix}
		\section{Simulation results}
		I modified the pole simulation so it's able to turn the full 360 degrees and tried to make the tabular q-learning agent learn to control it. At first it didn't work, so I decided to increase the size of the tables since the greedy action plot wasn't showing a clear pattern and it kept changing between runs. I ran it with 400 $\theta$, 400 $\dot{\theta}$ and I forgot how many options for the torque, but this was some 2 digit number. The greedy action for this case is shown in figure \ref{fig:ideal400}. At this point it became clear that my limits on $\dot{\theta}$ where to small and I increased it and ran it again in a smaller grid. Figure \ref{fig:ideal21} shows the ideal policy for an agent with 21 $\theta$, 11 $\dot{\theta}$ and 11 torque options.
